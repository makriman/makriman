# Ramakrishnan Lokanathan

I am an MBA candidate at London Business School, a mentor with the LBS AI Lab, and a member of the Data Science and AI Initiative. In 2026, I am also working on AI safety dataset quality review for NVIDIA Nemotron models and will spend the fall term at UC Berkeley on exchange.

Before LBS, I founded an applied AI product lab two months before ChatGPT launched, built early GPT-3/RAG products, took one of those products into AI-first pet commerce, and exited it to one of India's largest pet care businesses. I later led the post-acquisition digital division and scaled online revenue to about $7M.

My current interests sit around AI product infrastructure, retrieval and evaluation, AI-native enterprise software, learning systems, and the messy middle between research demos and products people actually use.

## The Through-Line

I started coding and building small websites early, including a quiz-answer checking model for an education project when I was 12. I later chose commerce, economics, and strategy because I wanted to build technology businesses, not only code in isolation.

That path has turned out to be useful in AI: the hard part is rarely just calling a model. It is knowing what to build around it, how to evaluate it, where it fails, how users behave, and how the system becomes a product.

I am now looking to work with teams building frontier AI products, research-adjacent tooling, and systems where strong product judgment and technical taste both matter.
